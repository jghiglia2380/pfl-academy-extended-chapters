### Economic Systems Coverage

**Capitalism (Market Economy):**
- Private ownership of resources
- Market price signals coordinate activity
- Profit incentives drive behavior
- Examples: Hong Kong, Singapore, pre-1930s USA
- Personal finance implications: High freedom, high opportunity, low security

**Socialism (Command Economy):**
- Government ownership of resources
- Central planning replaces markets
- Collective goals over individual profit
- Examples: Cuba, North Korea, former Soviet Union
- Personal finance implications: Low freedom, limited opportunity, basic security

**Mixed Economy:**
- Blend of market and government roles
- Most resources private, selective government intervention
- Balances efficiency with addressing market failures
- Examples: USA, Canada, most European countries
- Personal finance implications: Moderate freedom, significant opportunity, moderate security

## Core Concepts

**Key Insights:**
- Property rights enable investment, entrepreneurship, and wealth accumulation
- Market allocation generally produces higher prosperity than central planning
- Economic freedom correlates strongly with average income levels
- Mixed economies vary in their market-government balance
- Your economic system determines available financial opportunities

**Decision Framework:**
Before major financial decisions, consider:
- What does my economic system allow or restrict?
- What incentives does the system create?
- How do property rights protections affect this decision?
- What government programs or regulations apply?
