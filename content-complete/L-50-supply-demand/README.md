### Core Economic Concepts

**Law of Demand:**
- As price rises, quantity demanded falls (inverse relationship)
- Substitution effect, income effect, diminishing marginal utility
- Demand curve slopes downward
- Shifts from income changes, preferences, substitute prices

**Law of Supply:**
- As price rises, quantity supplied rises (positive relationship)
- Higher prices incentivize more production
- Supply curve slopes upward
- Shifts from technology, input costs, number of sellers

**Market Equilibrium:**
- Price where quantity demanded equals quantity supplied
- Shortages when price below equilibrium (quantity demanded > supplied)
- Surpluses when price above equilibrium (quantity supplied > demanded)
- Markets naturally move toward equilibrium through price adjustments

## Core Concepts

**Key Insights:**
- Prices signal scarcity: high prices mean high demand or low supply
- Market equilibrium balances competing forces automatically
- Predicting price changes requires identifying supply/demand shift factors
- Elasticity determines how much prices respond to changes
- Labor markets operate on same supply/demand principles as goods markets

**Decision Framework:**
Before major financial decisions, ask:
- Is this market's price at equilibrium, or temporarily high/low?
- What factors might shift supply or demand in the near future?
- How elastic is demand for this good (are there close substitutes)?
- Should I buy now or wait for better prices?
- For careers: Is demand for this skill rising or falling?
