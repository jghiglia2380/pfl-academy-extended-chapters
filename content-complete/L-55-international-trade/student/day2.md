## Activity 3: Career Planning in Global Economy (15 minutes)

### Part A: Job Vulnerability Assessment (8 minutes)

**Your intended career field:** _______________________

**Assess globalization risk:**

**Question 1: Can your job be done remotely?**
- ☐ Yes → Higher risk (competition from global workers)
- ☐ No → Lower risk (must be done locally)

**Question 2: Is your industry tradeable?**
- ☐ Manufacturing → High risk (outsourcing)
- ☐ Services (haircut, plumber) → Low risk (can't outsource)
- ☐ Knowledge work (coding, design) → Medium risk (depends on skills)

**Question 3: Automation risk?**
- ☐ Routine tasks → High risk (AI, robots replacing)
- ☐ Creative, interpersonal → Lower risk (harder to automate)

**Question 4: Comparative advantage?**
- Does US have advantage in your field?
- Advanced technology, innovation → Lower risk
- Commodity production → Higher risk

**Your overall globalization risk:**
- ☐ **High risk:** Job could move overseas or be automated
- ☐ **Medium risk:** Some risk but skills in demand
- ☐ **Low risk:** Must be done locally or US has strong advantage

**If high/medium risk, mitigation strategies:**

**Strategy 1: Develop non-tradeable skills**
- Communication, creativity, emotional intelligence
- These harder to outsource or automate

**Strategy 2: Specialize in high-value work**
- Advanced education, specialized expertise
- Be among the best globally

**Strategy 3: Embrace global opportunities**
- Work for multinational companies
- Remote work for global clients
- Export services globally

**Strategy 4: Choose growing sectors**
- Healthcare (aging population, must be local)
- Skilled trades (can't be done remotely)
- Technology (US has comparative advantage)
- Green energy (growing sector)

## Activity 4: Trade Impact Analyzer (Skill Builder) (10 minutes)

**Objective:** Simulate trade policy decisions and outcomes

**Scenario:** You're economic advisor to {{STATE_NAME}} governor

**Current Situation:**
- {{STATE_NAME}} unemployment: 4.5%
- {{STATE_NAME}} manufacturing jobs declining
- Consumers benefit from cheap imports
- Exporters facing foreign tariffs

**Policy Options:**

**Option 1: Free Trade (No Tariffs)**
- **Benefits:**
  - Lower consumer prices
  - More choices
  - Export growth (if partners reciprocate)
- **Costs:**
  - Some manufacturing jobs lost
  - Import-competing industries struggle

**Option 2: 25% Tariff on Imports**
- **Benefits:**
  - Protects {{STATE_NAME}} manufacturing jobs
  - Encourages domestic production
  - Government tariff revenue
- **Costs:**
  - Higher prices for consumers (+$3,000/year per household)
  - Retaliation (partners tariff our exports)
  - Economic inefficiency

**Option 3: Targeted Tariffs (Strategic Industries)**
- **Benefits:**
  - Protects critical sectors (national security)
  - Lower consumer impact than broad tariffs
- **Costs:**
  - Still raises some prices
  - Risk of retaliation
  - Difficult to choose which industries

**Your Recommendation:**
