# L-55: International Trade and Economic Development

## Chapter Overview

**Title:** International Trade and Economic Development
**Subtitle:** Understanding Global Economics and Your Role in the World Economy

This chapter explores international trade, comparative advantage, tariffs and trade policies, exchange rates, globalization, and economic development. Students learn how global economics affects their consumer choices, job prospects, and investment opportunities.

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, students will be able to:

1. Explain comparative advantage and why countries trade
2. Analyze benefits and costs of international trade (imports, exports, specialization)
3. Understand tariffs, quotas, and trade policies and their effects
4. Explain how exchange rates affect trade, travel, and investments
5. Assess how globalization affects job prospects and consumer choices
6. Evaluate trade policies and their impact on {{STATE_NAME}} industries
7. Develop strategies as global consumer and worker
8. Understand economic development and international investment opportunities

## Chapter Structure

### Day 1: Understanding International Trade (55 minutes)
- **Opening:** Why do you buy things made in other countries?
- **Comparative Advantage:** The foundation of trade
- **Benefits and Costs of Trade:** Consumer surplus vs. job displacement
- **Trade Policies:** Tariffs, quotas, and protectionism
- **Exchange Rates:** How currencies affect trade and travel
- **Discussion:** Free trade vs. protection

### Day 2: Globalization and Your Future - Learning Lab (55 minutes)
- **Activity 1:** {{STATE_NAME}} in the Global Economy
- **Activity 2:** Consumer Choices and Global Supply Chains
- **Activity 3:** Career Planning in a Global Economy
- **Activity 4:** Trade Impact Analyzer (Skill Builder)
- **Closing:** Your Global Economic Strategy

## Key Concepts

- **Comparative Advantage:** Producing what you're relatively better at
- **Absolute Advantage:** Producing more efficiently than others
- **Exports:** Goods sold to other countries
- **Imports:** Goods bought from other countries
- **Tariff:** Tax on imports
- **Quota:** Limit on import quantity
- **Exchange Rate:** Price of one currency in terms of another
- **Globalization:** Increasing economic integration across borders
- **Economic Development:** Process of improving living standards
