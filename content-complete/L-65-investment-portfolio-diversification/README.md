## Chapter Overview

L-65 provides comprehensive instruction on portfolio diversification theory and practice, teaching students how to construct balanced investment portfolios that reduce risk without sacrificing returns. Students learn Modern Portfolio Theory principles, understand correlation between asset classes, design age-appropriate allocation strategies, and implement disciplined rebalancing. This chapter transforms abstract investment concepts into practical skills for building and managing portfolios throughout their investing lifetime.

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this two-day chapter, students will be able to:

1. **Analyze how diversification reduces portfolio risk** through correlation principles
2. **Construct balanced investment portfolios** using appropriate asset allocation strategies
3. **Evaluate the relationship** between portfolio diversification and expected returns
4. **Apply rebalancing strategies** to maintain target asset allocation over time
5. **Design age-appropriate investment portfolios** aligned with time horizon and risk tolerance

## Chapter Structure

### Day 1: Understanding Portfolio Diversification (55 minutes)
**Format:** Traditional instruction with interactive skill builder

**Content Flow:**
- Introduction: "Don't put all eggs in one basket" principle applied to investing
- Key Concepts: 10 critical terms (diversification, asset allocation, correlation, MPT, efficient frontier, systematic vs. unsystematic risk, rebalancing, target-date funds, dollar-cost averaging)
- Deeper Exploration:
  - Mathematics of diversification and why it works
  - Asset classes and correlation patterns
  - Asset allocation strategies (60/40 and beyond)
  - Rebalancing principles and mechanics
  - Age-based portfolio design
  - Implications for wealth building
- Real-World Examples: Three investor scenarios
  - Aaliyah's automatic target-date strategy (simple, disciplined, successful)
  - Marcus's DIY diversification and crisis management (active, disciplined rebalancing through crash)
  - Destiny's undiversified lesson (concentration risk, panic selling, permanent losses)
- Reflection: Personal risk tolerance and allocation preferences
- Skill Builder: Portfolio Diversification Builder introduction

**Pedagogical Approach:**
Day 1 explains the mathematical and practical foundations of diversification, making abstract concepts concrete through real examples and visual demonstrations of correlation and allocation strategies.

### Day 2: Portfolio Building and Management Practice (55 minutes)
**Format:** Activity-based learning lab (90% activities, 10% instruction/reflection)

**Activities:**

1. **Correlation Detective Challenge (13 minutes)**
   - Analyze correlation matrices between asset classes
   - Build portfolios with different correlation profiles
   - Test diversification effects on risk and returns

2. **Age-Based Allocation Strategy Builder (15 minutes)**
   - Design appropriate allocations for investors aged 25, 45, and 60
   - Project retirement outcomes for different strategies
   - Understand time-risk relationship

3. **Market Crisis Survival Simulation (14 minutes)**
   - Experience concentrated vs. diversified portfolio performance through crash
   - Make decisions during crisis (panic sell vs. rebalance)
   - See long-term consequences of emotional vs. disciplined choices

4. **Rebalancing Practice Lab (8 minutes)**
   - Practice annual rebalancing decisions over 10-year period
   - Compare outcomes: your decisions vs. always rebalance vs. never rebalance
   - Learn mechanical "buy low, sell high" discipline

**Pedagogical Approach:**
Day 2 provides hands-on experience building portfolios, surviving crashes, and maintaining discipline through rebalancing, transforming theoretical knowledge into practical portfolio management skills.

### For Advanced Students:

### For Struggling Students:
- Simplify to 2-3 asset classes (stocks, bonds, cash)
- Use "120 minus age" formula for quick decisions

- Extensive visual aids (pie charts, graphs)
- Bilingual glossaries where available
- Translation tool access during activities
