---

**Your Personal Balance Sheet:**

**In High Interest Rate Environment (Current):**

**Costs:**
- Home: +$657/month = -$7,884/year
- Car: +$46/month = -$552/year
- Credit card: +$1,050 total
- **Total Cost:** ~$8,436/year in extra interest

**Benefits:**
- Savings: +$400/year

**Net Impact:** -$8,036/year

**Your Situation:**
- ☐ **Net borrower** (costs exceed benefits) - Hurt by high rates
- ☐ **Net saver** (benefits exceed costs) - Helped by high rates

**Most young people are NET BORROWERS** - High rates make it harder to:
- Buy first home
- Finance education
- Start business
- Build wealth

**But:** High rates necessary to control inflation. Your purchasing power protected.

**Trade-off:** Pay more interest now BUT your salary worth more (lower inflation)

## Closing Activity: Policy Philosophy (5 minutes)

**Reflect on your policy views:**

**1. Economic Stabilization:**

☐ **Activist:** Government should actively manage economy with fiscal and monetary policy
- Pros: Prevents recessions, maintains employment
- Cons: Government often gets it wrong, creates debt

☐ **Hands-off:** Government should minimize intervention, let markets self-correct
- Pros: Market efficiency, less debt
- Cons: Deeper recessions, more suffering

☐ **Middle ground:** Intervene in severe crises only

**2. Federal Reserve:**

☐ **Hawkish** (prioritize low inflation, accept higher unemployment)
☐ **Dovish** (prioritize low unemployment, accept higher inflation)
☐ **Balanced** (weight both equally)

**3. Fiscal Policy:**

☐ **Deficit spending acceptable** (borrow for stimulus when needed)
☐ **Balanced budgets required** (don't burden future generations)
☐ **Depends on circumstances** (deficit in recession, surplus in boom)
