## Key Features

### Educational Philosophy
**Central Message:** "Every financial choice has an opportunity cost—the value of the best alternative you give up. Understanding opportunity costs and incentives helps you make decisions that maximize value given your scarce resources."

**Practical Application:**
- Real dollar amounts in all calculations (no theoretical abstractions)
- Focus on major life decisions where opportunity cost analysis matters most
- Explicit vs. implicit cost distinction for comprehensive decision-making
- Incentive analysis connected to actual financial behaviors
- Time value considerations in all opportunity cost calculations

### Core Economic Concepts

**Scarcity:**
- Unlimited wants exceed limited resources (time, money, attention, energy)
- Forces choices and trade-offs in all economic decisions
- Applies universally regardless of income level
- Creates markets and price signals

**Opportunity Cost:**
- Value of next-best alternative foregone when making a choice
- Includes both explicit costs (direct payments) and implicit costs (foregone opportunities)
- Most important cost in financial decision-making
- Often exceeds direct monetary costs (especially time opportunity costs)

**Incentives:**
- Positive incentives (rewards) encourage behavior
- Negative incentives (penalties) discourage behavior
- Unintended consequences when incentives produce unexpected responses
- Design principle: align incentives with desired outcomes

## Core Concepts

**Key Insights:**
- Opportunity cost is the true cost of any decision, often exceeding monetary price
- Time is the ultimate scarce resource with significant opportunity cost
- Incentives shape behavior predictably—people respond to rewards and penalties
- Marginal analysis finds optimal amounts (not all-or-nothing)
- Sunk costs should be ignored in forward-looking decisions

**Decision Framework:**
Before major financial decisions, ask:
- What are all my alternatives (not just the two most obvious)?
- What is the opportunity cost of each alternative (explicit + implicit)?
- What incentives am I responding to, and are they aligned with my goals?
- Am I comparing marginal benefits to marginal costs correctly?
- Am I being influenced by sunk costs that should be ignored?
