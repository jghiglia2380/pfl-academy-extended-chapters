# L-61: Contract Evaluation and Consumer Protection - Chapter Summary

**Status:** ✅ COMPLETE

## Overview

L-61 teaches advanced contract analysis skills, moving beyond basic contract understanding to critical evaluation of unfair terms, consumer rights application, and negotiation strategies. Students learn to identify red flags, understand legal protections, and make informed decisions about contractual agreements.

**Usage:** Required by Louisiana (Topic j - Simple contracts, part 2); available for all states
**Focus:** Contract analysis, consumer rights, avoiding unfair agreements, negotiation

## Learning Objectives
1. Analyze contracts critically to identify favorable and unfavorable terms
2. Evaluate consumer rights and legal protections in contractual situations
3. Identify red flags and unfair contract terms
4. Apply contract evaluation strategies to real-world financial agreements
5. Make informed decisions about entering, negotiating, or declining contracts

## Structure

### Day 1: Contract Evaluation Fundamentals (55 minutes)
**Topics:**
- Unfair contract terms (one-sided rights, excessive penalties, broad waivers)
- Consumer protection laws (FTC Act, TILA, Magnuson-Moss, unconscionability)
- Red flag language ("as-is," "non-refundable," "binding arbitration")
- Systematic contract analysis (6-step framework)

**Real-World Examples:**
- Taylor's apartment lease negotiation success
- Marcus's gym membership trap
- Jordan's extended warranty evaluation

### Day 2: Contract Analysis Learning Lab (55 minutes)
**Activities:**
1. Real Contract Deep-Dive (15 min) - Analyze lease, gym, cell phone contracts
2. Consumer Rights Application (12 min) - Apply laws to scenarios
3. Red Flag Identification (10 min) - Pattern recognition practice
4. Negotiation Workshop (12 min) - Role-play and script development
5. Personal Contract Evaluation (11 min) - Apply to own contracts
